// 01 — About

More than just a hackathon

Hamilton Hacks is Hamilton College's first hackathon, bringing together students from all disciplines to tackle real-world problems through technology and creativity. Over 24 hours of building, learning, and collaborating, teams will build projects that change our world for the better. Whether you're a seasoned developer or picking up your first language, this is your time to work with peers to experiment boldly, and make something you're proud of!

// 03 — FAQ

Everything you need to know

Hamilton Hacks is Hamilton College's first hackathon where students from all backgrounds spend 24 hours building creative tech projects. No experience? No problem—we welcome all beginners!
Any currently enrolled Hamilton College student is eligible to participate. We welcome students of all majors and skill levels — you don't need to be a CS major to hack!
Not at all! Teams benefit from diverse skills — design, writing, research, and business thinking are just as valuable as coding. Workshops throughout the event will help beginners get up to speed.
Yes! Hamilton Hacks is completely free to participate in. We'll provide meals, snacks, and caffeine throughout the event.
Bring your laptop, charger, any hardware you plan to use, and something to sleep on if you're staying overnight and not planning on going back to your dorm. A water bottle and a positive attitude are also highly recommended.
Anything! Web apps, mobile apps, hardware projects, data visualizations, games — if you can build it in 24 hours, it's fair game. Specific tracks and challenges will be announced at the opening ceremony.
