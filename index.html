01 — About

More than just a hackathon

Hamilton Hacks is Hamilton College's first hackathon, bringing together students from all disciplines to tackle real-world problems through technology and creativity. Over 24 hours of building, learning, and collaborating, teams will create projects that change our world for the better. Whether you're a seasoned developer or picking up your first language, this is your time to experiment boldly and make something you're proud of.

03 — FAQ

Good questions

Hamilton Hacks is Hamilton College's first hackathon where students from all backgrounds spend 24 hours building creative tech projects. No experience? No problem — we welcome all beginners!
Any currently enrolled Hamilton College student is eligible to participate. We welcome students of all majors and skill levels — you don't need to be a CS major to hack!
Not at all! Teams benefit from diverse skills — design, writing, research, and business thinking are just as valuable as coding. Workshops throughout the event will help beginners get up to speed.
Yes! Hamilton Hacks is completely free. We'll provide meals, snacks, and caffeine throughout the event.
Bring your laptop, charger, any hardware you plan to use, and something to sleep on if staying overnight. A water bottle and a positive attitude are highly recommended.
Anything that connects to the Environment theme! Web apps, mobile apps, hardware, data visualizations — if you can build it in 24 hours, it's fair game. Specific tracks announced at the opening ceremony.
04 — Theme

This year's theme

Environment

From climate data to conservation tools, sustainable systems to biodiversity trackers — this year, we're building for the planet. Projects should engage with environmental challenges in a meaningful way, whether through direct solutions, awareness, education, or community action.

Sustainability
Energy, waste, food systems, circular economies
Climate
Data, modeling, adaptation, resilience
Biodiversity
Conservation, ecosystems, species, habitat
